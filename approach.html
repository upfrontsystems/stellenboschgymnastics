Our Approach

  1. Gymnastics as an essential part of Movement Education.
    • Early childhood movement education (3-6) essential gross motor skills development
    • Pre-school and primary school movement education (6-10) Dominant Mechanical Principles learning part of Physical Education.
  2. Gymnastics as a Cross Training Programme
    • Using the Dominant Mechanical Principles to all sports development
    • Using conditioning training (strength and flexibility training) to aid any sport code
    • Using basic gymnastics skills to aid technical work to any sport code (i.e. Soccer)
  3. Gymnastics as a competitive sport.
    • Gymnastics has nine disciplines: MAG, WAG, TRAMP, TUMB, ACRO, AEROBIC, RHYTMIC,ROPE SKIPPING,GYMNASTICS FOR ALL; (Maties has gymnasts practicing five of these disciplines.)
    • Talent Identification and Talent Nursing programmes
    • National and International competitive programmes and competitions
    • University Gymnastics programme
  4. Gymnastics as a lifelong activity
    • Gymnastics for life programmes (Adult Training) Gymnaestrada (every four years National and International participation opportunity with display work)
    • Gym for Life (also every four years National and International participation opportunity with display work )
  5. Gymnastics as an alternative Fitness Training method
    • Utilising conditioning (strength and flexibility training) methods combining with basic skill training as an alternative fitness training for anybody.
